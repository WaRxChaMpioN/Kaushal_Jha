About

I'm a PhD student in Hydrology at Colorado School of Mines, advised by Dr. Serveh Kamrava, working at the intersection of machine learning and computational fluid dynamics for subsurface flow. My current research focuses on building 3D CNN surrogates for lattice Boltzmann simulations in porous media — making flow prediction fast without solving the full physics. Best model: R² = 0.85 at 64³ resolution on Wendian HPC.

I hold a B.Tech (Mechanical Engineering) from AKTU and an M.Tech (Research) in Computational Mechanics from IIT Mandi (advised by Dr. Pradeep Kumar), where I developed coupled radiative-convective frameworks for volumetric solar receivers using MCRT and OpenFOAM.

Beyond the lab: I grew up in Delhi, but Himachal Pradesh is my second home — I've lived and explored the Himalayas more than anywhere else, and the mountains shaped how I think. When I'm not debugging HPC jobs on the Wendian cluster, I'm hiking the Colorado Rockies, lifting, or playing badminton. I believe being physically strong and intellectually sharp are the same discipline.

Research

My work spans porous media flow, radiative heat transfer, and applied ML for scientific computing.

Active · PhD Research

CNN Surrogate for 3D Porous Media Flow via Lattice Boltzmann Method

Reproducing and extending Wang et al. 2021 (Transport in Porous Media 138:49-75). A 3D CNN (K4N8 architecture) with a physics-informed continuity loss learns velocity fields from CT-scanned pore geometries — achieving R² = 0.85 at 64³ resolution on Wendian HPC. Enables fast, mesh-free flow prediction without full LBM solves.

ML-LBM 3D CNN Porous Media HPC TensorFlow

Current status: R² = 0.85 at 64³ resolution, ~510 epochs. Next step: continuity loss (alpha=0.1) from ckpt-16 at LR=1e-5. Dataset: 64³ porous geometries on Wendian HPC (/beegfs/scratch/kaushal_jha/ML_LBM_3D/). Target: reproduce Wang et al. 2021 Fig. 13 velocity field.

Under Review · Journal

Pore-Scale Radiation Modeling in TPMS-Based Volumetric Solar Receivers

Monte Carlo Ray Tracing (MCRT) in ANSYS Fluent and a homogenized OpenFOAM model for diamond-structured alumina TPMS absorbers. Characterizes radiation-conduction coupling and LTNE in concentrated solar receivers.

MCRT OpenFOAM TPMS Solar Energy Radiative Heat Transfer

MCRT simulation of diamond-structured Al₂O₃ TPMS absorbers. Homogenized OpenFOAM model captures LTNE between solid/fluid phases. Poster won first prize in thermal domain, IIT Mandi, Nov 2024. Resubmitted to IJHMT after 27 targeted revisions.

In Preparation · SPE Journal

ML-Based Pipeline Risk Classification for ECMC Gathering Flowlines

XGBoost pipeline for four-class pipeline risk classification (~15k flowlines, 24:1 class imbalance). BorderlineSMOTE + TomekLinks resampling, isotonic probability calibration, and Monte Carlo failure probability (μ = 2×MAOP, 10k samples) to generate physically grounded Low/Medium/High/Severe risk labels.

XGBoost SMOTE Risk Classification Pipeline Integrity Monte Carlo

15,331 ECMC gathering flowlines, 24:1 class imbalance. Failure probability via Monte Carlo: μ_Pf = 2×MAOP, σ_Pf = 0.1×μ_Pf, 10k samples. PR-AUC as primary metric. PHMSA as external validation.

Active · M.Tech Research

Tunnelling Through Fog: Laser Visibility via Monte Carlo Ray Tracing

Analysing complex refractive indices of ice and water across microwave, infrared, and visible spectra. Laser-fog interaction simulated using Volume-of-Fluid and Monte Carlo ray-tracing in ANSYS Fluent, with Mie theory scattering phase functions for realistic droplet modelling.

MCRT VOF ANSYS Fluent Mie Theory Optics

Complex refractive index analysis for ice and water across microwave, infrared, and visible spectra. Mie theory scattering phase functions for realistic fog droplet modeling.
