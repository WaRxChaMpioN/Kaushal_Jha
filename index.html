About

I came into research through mechanical engineering: first through thermodynamics and fluid mechanics, then through the uncomfortable realization that the most interesting engineering questions rarely fit inside a clean textbook boundary condition.

At IIT Mandi, that curiosity became radiation and porous media: Monte Carlo ray tracing, OpenFOAM, and TPMS solar absorbers where heat transfer, geometry, and numerical modeling all had to agree. That work taught me how much scientific progress depends on making simulations both physically faithful and computationally usable.

Now at Colorado School of Mines with Dr. Serveh Kamrava, I work on ML-CFD for subsurface flow. My focus is building 3D CNN surrogates that learn velocity fields from pore geometry and Lattice Boltzmann data, so researchers can explore flow behavior without paying the full cost of every CFD solve.

Research Vision

Long-term, I want to build trustworthy scientific ML systems for multiscale flow: models that are fast enough for screening and inverse design, accurate enough to respect physics, and transparent enough that hydrologists, CFD researchers, and engineers can use them with confidence.

Outside the lab: I grew up in Delhi, but Himachal Pradesh became the place that shaped how I think. Mountains made patience practical. When I am not debugging Wendian jobs, I am usually hiking in Colorado, lifting, or playing badminton.

Active · PhD Research

CNN Surrogate for 3D Porous Media Flow via Lattice Boltzmann Method

Problem
Full LBM simulations are too expensive for repeated pore-scale flow prediction across CT-derived geometries.
Approach
Train a 3D CNN surrogate on pore geometry and LBM velocity fields, then add continuity-aware loss terms.
Results
Best checkpoint: R² = 0.85 at 64³ resolution after roughly 510 epochs on Wendian HPC.
Impact
Enables fast flow-field screening without running a fresh CFD solve for every candidate geometry.
ML-LBM 3D CNN Porous Media HPC TensorFlow
GitHub Repos Manuscript Status

Architecture reference: Wang et al. 2021 K4N8-style 3D CNN for pore-scale velocity prediction. Current experiment path: /beegfs/scratch/kaushal_jha/ML_LBM_3D/. Next run: continuity loss alpha = 0.1 from ckpt-16 at LR = 1e-5, with field-level comparisons against published velocity slices.

Pore-Scale Radiation Modeling in TPMS-Based Volumetric Solar Receivers

Problem
Volumetric solar receivers need porous absorbers that capture radiation while controlling solid-fluid heat transfer.
Approach
Combine pore-scale MCRT in ANSYS Fluent with a homogenized OpenFOAM model for diamond TPMS absorbers.
Results
Journal manuscript resubmitted after 27 targeted revisions; poster won first prize in the thermal domain.
Impact
Connects geometry-resolved radiation physics to faster continuum-scale receiver analysis.

MCRT simulation of diamond-structured Al₂O₃ TPMS absorbers. Homogenized OpenFOAM model captures LTNE between solid and fluid phases. Validation emphasizes radiation-conduction coupling, effective thermal behavior, and receiver-scale transport relevance.

ML-Based Pipeline Risk Classification for ECMC Gathering Flowlines

Problem
Gathering flowline risk data are imbalanced, noisy, and difficult to convert into reliable integrity decisions.
Approach
Use XGBoost with BorderlineSMOTE, TomekLinks, isotonic calibration, and Monte Carlo failure probability.
Results
15,331 flowline records, 24:1 class imbalance, 10k Monte Carlo samples per failure-probability estimate.
Impact
Produces defensible Low/Medium/High/Severe labels for prioritizing pipeline integrity review.
XGBoost SMOTE Risk Classification Pipeline Integrity Monte Carlo
GitHub Repos Manuscript Status

15,331 ECMC gathering flowlines, 24:1 class imbalance. Failure probability via Monte Carlo: μ_Pf = 2×MAOP, σ_Pf = 0.1×μ_Pf, 10k samples. PR-AUC as primary metric. PHMSA as external validation.

Tunnelling Through Fog: Laser Visibility via Monte Carlo Ray Tracing

Problem
Fog reduces long-distance visibility through wavelength-dependent absorption and scattering.
Approach
Model refractive-index behavior for water and ice, then combine VOF, MCRT, and Mie phase functions.
Results
Mapped optical behavior across microwave, infrared, and visible regimes for droplet-scale simulations.
Impact
Supports better simulation of laser transmission in degraded-visibility environments.
